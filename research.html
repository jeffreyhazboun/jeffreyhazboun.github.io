Research Interests

My primary research interest is connecting theoretical predictions of gravity to astrophysical
phenomena through observational relativity. This broadly covers research in general relativity, the astrophysics of
strongly gravitating systems and using pulsar timing arrays (PTAs) as a tool to observe the universe through
gravitational wave astronomy. I am currently a senior postdoctoral fellow with the
North American Nanohertz Observatory for Gravitational Waves (NANOGrav).

The Stochastic Gravitational
Wave Background

This image above is titled Rough Seas a painting by Gerhard Mantz. The strongest signal of gravitational waves in the nanohertz band is expected to be the random superposition of waves from super-massive black holes in the cosmological neighborhood; similar to the sum of water waves from all directions out at sea. Detecting the stochastic background with PTAs will help us understand the interactions of super-massive blackholes withe their host galaxies and the history of galaxy mergers back through cosmic time.

Software Development

Modern astrophysics depends on large data sets, requiring sophisticated software for analysis. Many of my projects are software forward, developed as tools to benefit the entire community.

Noise Characterization and the Interstellar Medium

I study the myriad propagation effects to which the signals from millisecond pulsars are susceptible as they pass through interstellar space. I develop tailored, astrophysically motivated noise models so that it is easier to uncover the gravitational wave signals present in the timing data from pulsar timing arrays.
